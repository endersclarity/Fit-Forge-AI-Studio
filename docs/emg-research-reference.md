## PUSH EXERCISES

### Push-up (ex03)
- **Pectoralis major:** 70-80% MVIC
- **Triceps brachii:** 70-80% MVIC
- **Anterior deltoid:** 25-35% MVIC
- **Rectus abdominis:** 30-40% MVIC
- **Serratus anterior:** 40-50% MVIC
- **Citation:** Rodríguez-Ridao et al., 2020

### Dumbbell Bench Press (ex02)
- **Pectoralis major:** 85.7% MVIC
- **Anterior deltoid:** 21-26% MVIC
- **Triceps brachii:** 15% MVIC
- **Citation:** Rodríguez-Ridao et al., 2020

### Single Arm Dumbbell Bench Press (ex38)
- **Pectoralis major:** Similar to bilateral (~85% MVIC estimated)
- **Triceps brachii:** ~15% MVIC
- **Anterior deltoid:** ~21-26% MVIC
- **Core (anti-rotation):** Moderate-high (30-40% estimated)
- **Note:** Limited specific EMG data; extrapolated from unilateral pressing research

### Incline Dumbbell Bench Press (ex32)
- **Pectoralis major (clavicular):** 28-30% MVIC at 30°
- **Anterior deltoid:** 30-33% MVIC at 30-45°
- **Triceps brachii:** ~15% MVIC
- **Citation:** Rodríguez-Ridao et al., 2020
- **Note:** 30° incline produces maximal upper pec activation

### Single Arm Incline Dumbbell Bench Press (ex39)
- **Pectoralis major (clavicular):** 28-30% MVIC (similar to bilateral)
- **Anterior deltoid:** 30-33% MVIC
- **Triceps brachii:** ~15% MVIC
- **Core (anti-rotation):** Moderate-high (30-40% estimated)
- **Note:** Limited specific EMG research

### Dumbbell Shoulder Press (ex05)
- **Anterior deltoid:** 63.3% MVIC
- **Medial deltoid:** 27.9-30.3% MVIC
- **Pectoralis major:** 29.5-31.0% MVIC
- **Triceps brachii:** Moderate
- **Citation:** Saeterbakken et al., 2015; Campos et al., 2020

### Kettlebell Press (ex34)
- **Anterior deltoid:** 57.9% MVIC
- **Pectoralis major:** 29.5% MVIC
- **Serratus anterior:** Enhanced with bottom-up variation
- **Core:** Enhanced due to offset loading
- **Citation:** Saeterbakken et al., 2015

### TRX Pushup (ex31)
- **Pectoralis major:** 96.3-121.2% MVIC (feet suspended)
- **Triceps brachii:** 37.4-47.1% MVIC
- **Anterior deltoid:** Medium-high
- **Core:** Enhanced vs standard push-up
- **Citation:** TRX suspension research, 2025
- **Note:** +30°, +15°, and 0° angles show highest activation

### TRX Reverse Flys (ex29)
- **Posterior deltoid:** Moderate-high (specific % MVIC not documented)
- **Middle trapezius:** Moderate
- **Rhomboids:** Moderate
- **Note:** Limited TRX-specific EMG research; neutral grip increases posterior delt activation

### Tricep Extension (ex30)
- **Triceps brachii (long head):** 72-81% MVIC
- **Triceps brachii (lateral head):** 67-72% MVIC
- **Citation:** ACE Fitness Study, Porcari et al.
- **Note:** Overhead position emphasizes long head under stretch

### TRX Tricep Extension (ex40)
- **Triceps brachii:** Moderate-high (specific % MVIC not documented)
- **Core:** Moderate for stability
- **Anterior deltoid:** Light stabilization
- **Note:** Limited EMG research for TRX variation

### Dips (ex33)
- **Triceps brachii:** 87-88% MVIC
- **Pectoralis major:** 73-86% MVIC
- **Anterior deltoid:** Moderate
- **Citation:** ACE Study, McKenzie et al., 2022
- **Note:** Bar dips (1.04 mV) > bench dips (0.83 mV)

## PULL EXERCISES

### Pull-up (ex06)
- **Latissimus dorsi:** 117-130% MVIC
- **Biceps brachii:** 78-96% MVIC
- **Infraspinatus:** 71-79% MVIC
- **Lower trapezius:** 45-56% MVIC
- **Pectoralis major:** 44-57% MVIC
- **Citation:** Youdas et al., 2010

### Wide Grip Pull-ups (ex42)
- **Latissimus dorsi:** 117-130% MVIC
- **Middle trapezius:** 60.1% MVIC (peak)
- **Biceps brachii:** Lower than standard grip
- **Citation:** Dickie et al., 2017; Youdas et al., 2010
- **Note:** Wide grip = 60.1% mid traps vs neutral grip = 37.1%

- **Citation:** Youdas et al., 2010

### TRX Bicep Curl (ex19)

### Concentration Curl (ex22)

## LEG EXERCISES

- **Vastus lateralis:** 76.4% MVIC
- **Vastus medialis:** 67.8% MVIC
- **Gluteus maximus:** Moderate-high
- **Rectus femoris:** High

### Dumbbell Goblet Squat (ex43)
- **Vastus lateralis:** 76.4% MVIC
- **Vastus medialis:** 67.8% MVIC
- **Gluteus maximus:** Moderate-high
- **Rectus femoris:** High
- **Citation:** Same as kettlebell; patterns nearly identical

### Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (ex13)
- **Semitendinosus:** Higher than biceps femoris
- **Biceps femoris:** Moderate-high
- **Gluteus maximus:** Moderate (lower than conventional deadlift)
- **Erector spinae:** Significant (lower than conventional)
- **Citation:** Martín-Fuentes et al., 2020; Coratella et al., 2022

### Dumbbell Stiff Legged Deadlift (ex36)
- **Gluteus maximus:** Higher than Romanian deadlift
- **Semitendinosus:** Lower than Romanian deadlift
- **Biceps femoris:** Moderate-high
- **Erector spinae:** High
- **Citation:** Coratella et al., 2022

### Box Step-ups (ex47)
- **Gluteus maximus:** 169.22% MVIC (standard step-up)
- **Vastus lateralis:** 62-72% MVIC
- **Rectus femoris:** 40-63% MVIC
- **Gluteus medius:** High
- **Citation:** Rebuttal et al., 2020; Youdas et al.
- **Note:** Among highest glute activation of all exercises

### Calf Raises (ex15)
- **Gastrocnemius medialis:** 50-52% MVIC
- **Gastrocnemius lateralis:** 49-51% MVIC
- **Soleus:** 50-51% MVIC
- **Citation:** Soares et al., 2020; André et al., 2021

### Glute Bridges (ex35)
- **Gluteus maximus:** 16.8-33.0% MVIC (bodyweight), 65-86% MVIC (loaded)
- **Biceps femoris:** 16.5-29.8% MVIC
- **Citation:** Hollman et al., 2018
- **Note:** Verbal/tactile cueing increases activation from 16.8% to 33.0%

### Kettlebell Swings (ex37)
- **Biceps femoris:** 78.95% MVIC (mean)
- **Gluteus maximus:** 75.02% MVIC (mean)
- **Medial hamstrings:** 115% MVIC (peak)
- **Gluteus medius:** 55.47% MVIC
- **Citation:** Lake & Lauder, 2014; McGill & Marshall
- **Note:** Hip hinge style produces 115% MVIC peak in medial hamstrings

## METHODOLOGY NOTES

**Sample Sizes:** Most studies n=10-30 participants
**Load Prescription:** 60-80% 1RM or 6-10RM typical
**Values >100% MVIC:** Common in dynamic exercises (isometric MVIC testing vs dynamic movement)
**Individual Variability:** Standard deviations often ±20-50% MVIC
**Citations:** 189 peer-reviewed sources (see Perplexity research output for full bibliography)

## USAGE GUIDE

**For FitForge Implementation:**
- Primary muscles: Typically >60% MVIC
- Secondary muscles: 40-60% MVIC
- Supporting muscles: 20-40% MVIC
- Minimal involvement: <20% MVIC

**Confidence Levels:**
- ✅ High confidence: Specific % MVIC from peer-reviewed studies
- ⚠️ Moderate confidence: Estimated from similar exercises
- ❌ Low confidence: Limited research available
